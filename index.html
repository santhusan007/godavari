About Gokarna

Located in the southwestern Indian state of Karnataka,Gokarna is a popular pilgrimage destination for Hindu’s and known for its pristine beaches,

...

breath taking landscapes and rich history. Gokarna welcomes tourists from around the world in search of sanctity and relaxation. The windy streets and alley ways enchants tourists and pilgrims capturing the spiritual ambience of the temples where prayers and piety take place daily. The temples in Gorkana are a site to endeavour. The Mahabaleshwara temple built in the 4th century AD, the temple is the prominent place of worship for pilgrims. Built by King Mayurasharma of the Kadamba Dynasty with white granite stone and gopuras within the temple grounds and statue of Lord Shiva. A 6 ft shiva Linga here at the temple is known as the Atmalinga is worshipped here. As well as Mahabaleshwara temple pilgrims also come to visit many of the other temples which include Maha Ganapati shrine, Chandikeswara, Thamira Gowri (Goddess Parvati shrine) where Muktishetra take place, and Aadi Gokarneswara, Dattatreya, and Gokarnanayagi.welcomes hordes of tourists from around the world in search of sanctity and relaxation.

It is also known for the seven Muktishetras of the Hindus, where they come to perform the last rites.

Things to do in gokarna

With its beautiful beaches and green palms Gorkana offers visitors the chance to relax and enjoy sea, sand and sun in one location. Whether you wish to swim, sunbathe or surf on the waves, Gorkana beaches have ample facilities to cater for all needs. Om and Kudle

...

beach are merely a stone throw away (5 and 6 km) from The Godaveri making it the ideal spot for families, couples, pilgrims, and groups of friends to escape and relax. The Godaveri offers excursions and tours as well as travel arrangements to these popular beaches. If you prefer something quieter then a cruise on the back waters will encapulate the essence of Gorkana. With tranquillity and beautiful nature Gorkana’s back water is filled with luscious green rivers.

With The Godavari hotel we can whisk you away to see the spectacular water falls of Jog falls which is a day trip along with the Vibhuti fall which is 40 km away from Gorkana. These water fall have been around since time began and the earth’s gradual formation of rocks and waterfalls.

In the low hills and paddy fields of Mundgod lies the Tibetan Monastery Colony. The Monastery welcomes thousands of monks to complete their theological studies and pray here and if you are lucky, you might hear the chanting of prayers.

There are several other monasteries which you can visit and explore the town which offers delicious traditional Tibetan food and you must try the thukpa!

With our guest relation at The Godavari we can prepare a bespoke package for your trip.

For keen botanist’s you must visit the Karwar Rock Gardens where history of specific communities come alive in the form of life size models of different communities of North Karnataka such as Sidhis, Goulis, Kunabis, Gondas and the Halakki are displayed. You will also glimpse the statues of the Yakshgana dance which is the famous classical dance of Gorkana as well as other classical and folk dances.

If its adrenalin you seek then why not try the water sports that are on offer here such as, banana boats, small boat cruises on and even white water rafting along the lush river, traditional boating with the opportunity to fish with the local fishermen early hours of the morning.

Bird watching on Paradise beach, half moon beach, Om and Kudle beach (specific timings for bird watching).
